Solar System

         The Solar System[b] is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it. Of the bodies that orbit the 
 Sun directly, the largest are the four gas and ice giants and the four terrestrial planets, followed by an unknown number of dwarf planets and 
 innumerable small Solar System bodies. Of the bodies that orbit the Sun indirectly—the natural satellites—two are larger than Mercury, the 
 smallest terrestrial planet, and one is nearly as large.[c]
